<h3>Vision and Mission</h3>
<p>In Yishun Secondary School, the Student Leadership programme aims to contribute
to the development of our students as Capable, Creative, and Compassionate
Leaders. Our student leaders are empowered to embody the school’s spirit
through their daily actions and influence. By nurturing these leaders to
exemplify our school values, we prepare them to actively contribute to
society, ensuring they play a tangible role in improving the lives of those
in the community they serve.</p>
<h3>A Tiered Approach to Leadership Development</h3>
<p>The Student Leadership Curriculum is designed around the Five Practices
in Kouzes and Posner’s ‘The Leadership Challenge’. Structured as a progressive
journey, our curriculum is designed to meet the developmental needs of
our students at every level. Students are given opportunities to develop
attributes and deepen their learning at various platforms.</p>
<h3>Secondary 1: Leader of Self</h3>
<p>Through self-discovery modules incorporated into the Secondary 1 Camp,
students are challenged to develop personal independence and resilience.</p>
<h3>Secondary 2: Leader of Others</h3>
<p>Building upon personal mastery, the Secondary 2 leadership programme shifts
toward preparing students for the complexities of peer influence and team
dynamics. It equips them with key competencies to take on expanded responsibilities
as they transit into leadership roles in the upper secondary years.</p>
<h3>Upper Secondary</h3>
<p>Students are appointed to leadership positions within the Prefectorial
Board, Peer Support Leaders and CCA executive committee. Our student leaders
engage in meaningful learning experiences and spearhead initiatives supported
by teacher mentors that serve the wider school community.</p>
<h3>Class Committee</h3>
<p>At the heart of our school’s vibrant student life is the Class Committee,
a dedicated team of student leaders committed to fostering a positive classroom
environment and strengthening class spirit.</p>
<p>Class Monitors are the student representatives of each class. They are
the bridge between the teachers and their classmates. They maintain a positive
learning environment and are key communicators to uphold class unity.</p>
<p>CCE Champions advocate for our school values by leading class Values-in-action
(VIA) projects and spearhead National Education commemorative events.</p>
<p>Eco-Stewards drive sustainability efforts through school-wide and community
environment initiatives such as Green Week and Green Day in the community
to foster an eco-conscious community.</p>
<p>Health Champions advocate for healthy and active living. They support
PE lessons and facilitate major school sporting events like Cross-country
and Inter-class games.</p>
<h3>CCA Leaders</h3>
<p>Our CCA leaders are selected by their teachers following a period of observation.
The CCA leaders are provided with diverse developmental platforms by their
teachers-in-charge and CCA coaches, tailored to hone the specific key competencies
required for their respective CCA. CCA leaders are key to nurturing a deep
sense of belonging and team spirit. By mentoring their juniors and modeling
excellence, they create a supportive yet disciplined environment where
character and skill are developed.</p>
